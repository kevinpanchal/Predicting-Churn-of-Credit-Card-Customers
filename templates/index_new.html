Fundamentals of Credit Card

Before we start technical implementation it is absolutely essential that we understand how credit card ecosystem function as a business. For customer it is about making credit history with some convience and rewards. It is easy for credit card customer to show their record and leverage their credibility for any new purchase. In simple term credit card is short term loan for card issuer. With opening up your credit card account, company gives you credit limit in which you can pay bills and do purchases, on this loan company charge you some interest rate and if you miss repayment your penalty charge is rising. When customer do payment with credit card, request goes to card issuing company trough processor and card network. Card issuing company process data with pre recorded data and give approval to the processor. By request of merchant, approved money goes to mechant bank from processor. In the end of credit cycle, card issuing company give bill to their customer. All the data set and procedure which we are doing in this project is a part of card issuing bank.

New credit card billing rules effective from July 1

Card-issuers shall ensure that there is no delay in sending/dispatching/emailing bills/statements and the customer has sufficient number of days (at least one fortnight) for making payment before the interest starts getting charged. In order to obviate frequent complaints of delayed billing, the card-issuer may consider providing bills and statements of accounts through internet/mobile banking with the explicit consent of the cardholder.

The RBI’s new credit cards rules explained

The directions earmark which banks may engage in the credit and debit card business. Most Scheduled Commercial Banks (SCBs) with a net worth of Rs. 100 crores can issue credit cards. The exception is Regional Rural banks (RRBs) which need to collaborate with other banks to do so.
